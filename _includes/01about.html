About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Virginia Tech. Experimental Economics and Behavioral Economics are my main research interests. My experimental work focuses on the sequential multi-battle contest that models the primary elections in the United States. On the other hand, my research on behavioral models covers various topics such as time preference and risk preference.

I hold a master's degree in Economics from Virginia Tech, a master's degree in Statistics from the University of California, Santa Barbara, and a bachelor's degree in Economics from Kansas State University. The background colors of this website are the Royal Purple from K-Sate, the Navy, Gold from UC Santa Barbara, and the Chicago Maroon, Burnt Orange from Virginia Tech.
